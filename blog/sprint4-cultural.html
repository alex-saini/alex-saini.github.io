Neuroplasticity

How does understanding neuroplasticity help people?

Noticing the positive effects that neuroplasticity can have on a person ultimitely change how a person works, studies or goes about their general day. If people can understand the benefits of re-wiring their brain with healthy and positive habits, they can lead a healthier lifestyle while also seeing major improvement in whatever they train their brain to do. Improved focus, memory, happier mind! Personally, I will be taking small steps in order to gain certain goals / research certain methods that help grow a stronger and more focused mindset.


What are some ways to increase neuroplasticity?

Immersing yourself into new challenges. Following a plan and keeping to it. But three written ways I have found are these:

  • Setting meaningful goals
  • Practicing mindfulness regularly
  • Adopting a growth mindset lifestyle

Growth Mindset

What is Growth Mindset and how have I been interacting with it?

Growth Mindset is the belief that you can develop your talents through *hard work, good strategies and input from others. It is the opposite of a fixed mindset - which enables the beliefs of "something is too hard so I give up" or taking the easier route. Growth Mindset is constantly challenging yourself to enable strong neuro connections, it is the belief of constant learning and forever pushing yourself.

Personally I don't think I have been engaging with these beliefs enough, I had a week and a bit off from study and getting back into a routine and rhythm is both faustrating and difficult. But learning some of the small neuroplasticity principals and working with them I think will help get me into a concrete work routine which can hopefully be build on the foundations of a growth mindset.
